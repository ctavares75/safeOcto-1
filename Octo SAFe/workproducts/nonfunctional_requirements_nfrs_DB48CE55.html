Artifact: Nonfunctional Requirements (NFRs)
Nonfunctional Requirements (NFRs) define system attributes such as security, reliability, performance, maintainability, scalability, and usability. They serve as constraints or restrictions on the design of the system across the different backlogs.

Description
Main DescriptionAlso konwn as system qualities, nonfuntional requirements are just as critical as functional Epics, Capabilities, Feautres, and Stories. They ensure the usability and effectiveness of the entire system. NFRs are persistent quialities and constraints that, unlike functional requirements, are typically revisited as part of the Definition of Done (DoD) for each Iteration, Program Increment (PI), or release. NFRs exist in all backlogs: Team, Program, Solution, and Portfolio.
Key Considerations
Many NFRs prescribe that some additional work must be done to satisfy them. Sometimes the NFR must be implemented all at once; other times the teams can take a more incremental approach.
