Role: Product Owner
The Product Owner (PO) is a member of the Agile Team responsible for defining Stories and prioritizing the Team Backlog to streamline the execution of program priorities while maintaining the conceptual and technical integrity of the Features or components for the team.
Role Sets: Agile PMO, Agile Portfolio Operations, Agile Release Train (ART), Lean Agile Center of Excellence (LACE), Lean Governance , Roles
Relationships
Primary Performs
  • Accepting Stories
  • Contribution to the Solution Demo
  • Contribution to the System Demo
  • Iteration Planning
  • Just-In-Time Story Elaboration
  • Maintaining the Team Backlog
  • Participating in Team Demo and Retrospective
  • Program Backlog Refinement
  • Program Increment (PI) Planning
  • Supporting Acceptance Test-Driven Development (ATDD)
  • Understanding Enabler Work
Main Description
The PO has a significant role in quality control and is the only team member empowered to accept stories as done. This role which serves as a Customer proxy has significant relationships and responsibilities outside the local team, including working with Product Management, who is responsible for the Program Backlog, to prepare for the Program Increment (PI) Planning meeting. This allows the Solution to effectively address program priorities (features and Enablers) while maintaining technical integrity. Ideally, the PO is collocated with the rest of the team, where they typically share management, incentives, and culture. But the PO also attends most relevant Product Management meetings about planning and Program Backlog/Vision refinement.
