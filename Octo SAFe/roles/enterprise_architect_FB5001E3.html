Role: Enterprise Architect
The Enterprise Architect promotes adaptive design, and engineering practices and drives architectural initiatives for the portfolio. Enterprise Architects also facilitate the reuse of ideas, components, services, and proven patterns across various solutions in a portfolio.
Extends: Enterprise Architecture
Role Sets: Lean Governance , Roles, Strategy and Investment Funding
Relationships
Primary Performs
  • Collaborating with Lean Portfolio Management
  • Defining Key Technical Initiatives
  • Driving Architectural Initiatives
  • Facilitating the Reuse of Code
  • Generating and Analyzing Innovative Ideas and Technolgies
  • Influencing Common Modeling, Design, and Coding Practices
  • Planning and Developing the Architectural Runway
  • Promoting Continuous Delivery Pipeline and DevOps Capabilities
  • Synchronizing the Following Disciplines Across Solutions
  • Understanding and Communicating Strategic Themes
Main Description
Enterprise Architects work with business stakeholders and Solution and system Architects to implement technology initiatives across Value Streams. They rely on continuous feedback, foster adaptive design and engineering practices, and drive programs and teams around to ally a common technical vision.
Staffing
SkillsRespect for people, relentless improvement
Assignment ApproachesCould potentially be considered for a role in Shared Services. Shared Services represents the specialty roles, people, and services that are necessary for the success of an Agile Release Train (ART) or Solution Train but that cannot be dedicated full-time.
Key Considerations
Developers and testers will better trust strategy driven by the person who knows their current challenges and context. Likewise, the Enterprise Architect will better trust teams that provide full visibility of their current context.
