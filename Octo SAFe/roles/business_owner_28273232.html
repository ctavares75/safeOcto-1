Role: Business Owner
Business Owners are a small group of stakeholders who have the primary business and technical responsibility for governance, compliance, and return on investment (ROI) for a Solution developed by an Agile Release Train (ART). They are key stakeholders on the ART who must evaluate fitness for use and actively participate in certain ART events.
Role Sets: Agile Release Train (ART), Business Owners, Roles, Strategy and Investment Funding
Relationships
Primary Performs
  • Actively Addressing Impediments
  • Assigning Business Value
  • Inspect and Adapt Workshops
  • Participating in Post-PI Planning
  • Participating in Pre-PI Planning
  • Playing a Primary Role in Draft Review
  • Specification Workshops
Main Description

Business Owners can be identified by asking the following questions:

• Who is ultimately responsible for business outcomes?

• Who can steer this ART to develop the right solution?

• Who can speak to the technical competence of the solution now and into the near future?

• Who should participate in planning, help eliminate impediments, and speak on behalf of development, the business, and the customer?

• Who can approve and defend a set of Program Increment (PI) plans, knowing full well that they will never satisfy everyone?

• Who can help coordinate the efforts with other departments and organizations within the enterprise?

Staffing
SkillsSelf-management, self-organization
Assignment ApproachesSAFe defines the responsibilities of Business Owners as the key managers who guider the ART to the appropriate outcomes. The reccomended activities for Business Owners in SAFe enable them to fulfill their obligations to the enterprise while empowering the teams to do their best work.
Key Considerations
An effective Business Owner is an active and involved Business Owner, fulfilling SAFe responsibilities daily. Active participation of Business Owners is critical to the success of the train. Business Owners may participate, in some cases, in Lean Portfolio Management (LPM), Product Management, and System Architecture, and serve as an Epic Owner, where appropriate.
