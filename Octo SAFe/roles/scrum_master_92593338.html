Role: Scrum Master
Scrum Masters are servant leaders and coaches for an Agile Team. They help educate the team in Scrum, Extreme Programming (XP), Kanban, and SAFe, ensuring that the agreed Agile process is being followed. They also help remove impediments and foster an environment for high-performing team dynamics, continuous flow, and relentless improvement.
Role Sets: Agile Portfolio Operations, Agile Release Train (ART), Communities of Practice (CoP), Lean Agile Center of Excellence (LACE), Lean Governance , Roles
Relationships
Primary Performs
  • Building a High-Performing Team
  • Coordinating With Other Teams
  • Eliminating Impediments
  • Exhibiting Lean-Agile Leadership
  • Facilitating Meetings
  • Facilitating Preparation and Readiness for ART Events
  • Facilitating the Team's Progress Toward Team Goals
  • Leading the Team Efforts in Relentless Improvement
  • Promoting SAFe Quality Practices
  • Protecting and Communicating
  • Responsibilities on the Train
  • Supporting Estimates
  • Supporting Product Owner
  • Supporting the Team Rules
Additionally Performs
  • Contribution to the System Demo
  • Inspect and Adapt Workshops
  • Program Increment (PI) Planning
Main Description
The Scrum Master role is a unique Agile team member who spends much of their time helping other team members communicate, coordinate, and cooperate; generally, this person assists the team in meeting their delivery goals.
Staffing
SkillsLean-Agile leadership, relentless improvement
Key Considerations
SAFe takes a pragmatic approach and assumes, in general, that the Scrum Master is a part-time role. During initial SAFe adoption, however, the job may be more intensive.
